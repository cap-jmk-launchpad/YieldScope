# Why earn-only beats balance sheets for monthly rewards questions

## The wrong tool for the question

“What did I make this month?” is not the same question as “What is my portfolio worth?” Spot balances swing with price. Trade PnL mixes intent. Tax software wants every transfer. None of those surfaces are optimized for **rewards** — Simple Earn interest, OKX savings accruals, staking payouts.

## What an earn-only ledger keeps

Only rows that represent income from earning products or staking: interest credited, rewards claimed or pending, Auto Earn amounts. Principal deposits stay out. Unrealized mark-to-market stays out. That is YieldScope’s Phase 1 job.

## Why that matters for scattered rewards

When rewards live in four UIs, people paste numbers into a spreadsheet and lose the provenance. An earn-only ledger keeps source, time, and amount so the monthly total is auditable — and optionally checkpointed on Monad.

## Scope honesty

Phase 1 covers Binance Simple Earn, OKX savings/earn, Monad staking, and LUNC. Voice can say “rewards”; the product does not claim every chain or a tax suite.
